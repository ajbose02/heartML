You are at risk for heart disease.

Although you are currently at risk, you can still reduce your chances by improving your health. 
Here are some ways you can reduce your risk: 

Maintain a healthy diet

Reduce fat intake, increase fiber-intake. Your diet should include 5 servings of fresh fruit and vegetables and whole grains.

Increase physical activity

Regular exercise is the best way to maintian a healthy weight. Increasing physical activity lowers cholestrol levels and maintains blood pressure levels. 

Prioritize sleep

Most adults need at least 7 hours of sleep per night. Set a sleep schedule to reduce risk of obesity, high blood pressure, diabetes and depression. 

Reduce alcohol intake

Do not drink more than 14 units a week. Avoiding alcohol altogether is the best way to reduce risk of heart disease.

Quit smoking

Smoking is a major risk for developing heart disease. By quitting smoking, you will significantly improve your general health.

Control blood pressure

You should target a blood pressure below 140/90mmHg. Check your blood pressure regularly and maintain a healthy diet.
